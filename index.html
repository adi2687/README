What is the Internet?

The Internet is a global network of interconnected computers and computer networks that use the Internet Protocol Suite (TCP/IP) to communicate between networks and devices. It carries a vast range of information resources and services, including the inter-linked hypertext documents and applications of the World Wide Web (WWW).

Key Components

  • Servers: Powerful computers that store and deliver web content
  • Clients: Devices (like your computer or phone) that request and display content
  • Routers: Devices that direct internet traffic between networks
  • ISPs (Internet Service Providers): Companies that provide internet access to users
  • DNS (Domain Name System): The phonebook of the internet, translating domain names to IP addresses

How the Internet Works

When you enter a website address in your browser:

  1. Your request is sent to your ISP
  2. The request is routed through various networks to the correct server
  3. The server processes the request and sends back the requested data
  4. Your browser receives and displays the website

This entire process typically takes just milliseconds!

Internet Statistics (as of 2023)

  • Over 5.3 billion people use the internet worldwide
  • Approximately 1.13 billion websites exist on the internet
  • Google processes over 8.5 billion searches per day
  • Over 300 billion emails are sent daily
